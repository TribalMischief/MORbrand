Current State
An honest diagnostic of MOR Wealth Management's brand as it exists today.
Brand Summary

MOR Wealth Management presents as a values-driven, independent RIA based in Center City Philadelphia. The brand communicates professionalism and trustworthiness through conventional wealth management aesthetics — clean, conservative, understated — while layering in progressive differentiators (B Corp certification, SRI focus, Triple Bottom Line philosophy) that are clearly meaningful to the firm but largely invisible in the brand's visual language. The result is a firm that sounds different in writing but looks like every other wealth manager on the block.

Current Typography

No custom typefaces are consistently defined in brand materials. The site uses standard web fonts — likely a clean sans-serif or generic Google Font — without a defined hierarchy or pairing system. There is no evidence of a display typeface chosen to express brand personality. Typography, like color, is undefined rather than considered.

Current Logo & Mark

The logo is a wordmark — "MOR" in a clean, simple treatment used with "Wealth Management" as a descriptor. A white-reversed version exists for dark backgrounds. The wordmark communicates: established, simple, safe. It does not communicate: values-driven, B Corp certified, socially responsible, Philadelphia-rooted, or independent. The mark does not distinguish MOR from the national wirehouses it positions itself against — which is a significant missed opportunity given how meaningfully the firm's philosophy differentiates it.

The brand has two distinct voices operating in parallel without a system connecting them. The blog is warm, witty, and conversational — a trusted friend who happens to manage your money. The website is formal and aspirational. Neither voice is wrong; the problem is that they don't clearly belong to the same brand. A new client reading the homepage, then clicking to the blog, would wonder if they've navigated somewhere else.

Identified Gaps & Inconsistencies
  • B Corp certification — one of the most powerful differentiators available to any wealth manager — appears in navigation as a single page rather than woven through the brand's visual identity, voice, and positioning
  • No defined color palette — colors appear to derive from stock financial-industry templates, not from brand strategy
  • No defined typographic system — no display/body pairing, no hierarchy rules, no personality expressed through typeface choice
  • Brand voice is split: blog reads warm and witty; website reads formal and aspirational; no codified voice bridges the gap
  • The "Triple Bottom Line" (People, Planet, Profit) is stated verbally but has zero visual expression — it's a brand platform sitting unused
  • Tagline ("Socially Responsible Investment Management, Estate Planning and Family Wealth Management") is a service descriptor, not a brand promise — it tells you what they sell, not why it matters
  • The "Our Family" page framing — not "Our Team" — is a meaningful brand choice signaling warmth and community, but it's not reinforced visually or tonally elsewhere
  • Philadelphia identity is referenced (address, community involvement, 1,200+ volunteer hours from the CEO alone) but not developed as a brand asset
  • 10 years of "Giving with Purpose" events demonstrates values rather than merely stating them — it's dramatically underrepresented as a differentiator
  • B Lab, the organization that governs B Corp certification, was founded in Wayne, PA — a Philadelphia suburb. MOR practices these values in the movement's literal hometown. This story is invisible in the brand.
Overall Impression

If you encountered MOR Wealth Management cold — without reading their copy, without knowing about the B Corp certification, without seeing the team bios — you would see a clean, unremarkable financial services firm. The visual brand does not do the firm's values justice. MOR has a genuine and differentiated philosophy: independent, B Corp certified, triple bottom line, community-rooted, Philadelphia-based, ten-year track record of purpose-driven giving. All of it is hidden behind generic presentation. The gap between what MOR believes and how MOR looks is the central problem to solve.

Direction 02 — Refined & Consistent
MOR Wealth Management
What the existing brand could be with intention and consistency applied
The Refinement Thesis

MOR already has the right instincts — the warmth, the values, the team culture, the community commitment. What the brand lacks is a system that expresses those instincts consistently. This direction honors every meaningful choice MOR has already made (the "Our Family" framing, the approachable blog voice, the SRI focus) and gives them a visual language worthy of the philosophy they practice. The core insight: sustainability and wealth are not opposites. Deep forest green has long signaled both growth and responsibility. This is not a rebrand — it's a promotion of what MOR already is.

Inspiration

B Lab — the organization that created and governs the Certified B Corporation standard — was founded in Wayne, Pennsylvania, a suburb of Philadelphia, in 2006. MOR Wealth Management practices B Corp values in the movement's literal hometown. This is not a coincidence; it's a lineage. Philadelphia has always been a city where ethical commerce was treated as a civic obligation — from William Penn's founding vision of principled governance to a centuries-long business tradition that viewed profit and principle as inseparable. This direction draws from that deep vein: not "old Philadelphia" as an aesthetic affectation, but the specific seriousness of a city that has always believed wealth could be a vehicle for good. The brand that emerges doesn't shout its values — it embodies them the way a well-made document does. Principled. Permanent. Built to last.

Logo & Mark Concept

The bold direction proposes a structural departure: the "MOR" wordmark set in Libre Baskerville small caps, generous tracking (0.2em) — a treatment that looks architectural, like a firm name etched in stone. Below: "Wealth Management" in Source Sans 3 caps at 0.25em tracking. To the left of the wordmark: the triple rule mark — three horizontal bars of ascending length (short, medium, long) in Brass, representing the Triple Bottom Line: People, Planet, Profit. The mark is minimal and tells the brand story without a word. An alternate version uses the triple rule mark alone — without the wordmark — for contexts where the brand is established (stationery backs, apparel, environmental signage).

Direction 04 — The Synthesis
Wealth with Purpose.
Refinement and bold thinking, combined
The Synthesis

This direction takes the visual refinement of Tab 2 — forest green, elevated typography, B Corp-forward identity — and sharpens it with the principled conviction of Tab 3's Philadelphia heritage angle. The triple rule mark from Tab 3 becomes the defining identity element, giving the brand a visual anchor that tells its story silently. The tagline is "Wealth with Purpose" — a phrase MOR has essentially been living for a decade without naming. For MOR right now, this is the right move: the firm is mature enough for an elevated visual system, differentiated enough to own a principled position, and Philadelphia-rooted enough that leaning into that lineage is authentic, not borrowed.

The mark sits left of the wordmark in horizontal lockup, or above in stacked lockup. The B Corp certification badge is treated as a co-identifier on business cards, the website header, and all email signatures — a credential displayed with the same visibility as CFA and CFP® designations. Three official versions: Forest on Ivory (primary), White on Forest (reversed), single-color Ink (print/embossing).

Implementation — First 3 Moves
  1. Put the B Corp certification front-and-center today. Update the homepage hero to lead with "Certified B Corp · Wealth with Purpose" before any service description. Add the B Corp co-identifier to all email signatures and the website footer immediately — this costs nothing and signals a brand with conviction.
  2. Lock the color system. Implement Forest (#1B4332), Ivory (#F5F1EB), and Brass (#B5892A) across the website in a single design pass. Replace the generic navy. This one change — before any logo or copy work — will make the existing content look significantly more intentional and premium.
  3. Write a one-page voice guide. Codify the two-register rule (formal vs. conversational contexts), the five voice adjectives, and the tagline. Share it with every person who writes for MOR. The blog voice is already excellent — the goal is to give it permission to exist on the website, appropriately modulated.

The Bottom Line

MOR Wealth Management has spent a decade building something genuinely rare in financial services: a profitable, B Corp certified, values-led firm with a real community of clients who believe in what they're doing. The brand currently hides all of that behind aesthetics that belong to the wirehouses they left. The recommended direction — Tab 4 — gives the firm a visual system that finally matches the philosophy: the triple rule mark as a permanent, silent declaration of the Triple Bottom Line; "Wealth with Purpose" as the tagline MOR has been living without naming; and forest green that signals sustainability and conviction rather than inherited financial conservatism. The Departure (Tab 3) should be kept in view — if MOR ever wants to plant a flag nationally as the B Corp wealth manager, the Philadelphia heritage direction is the foundation for that story. For now, Tab 4 is the right move: ambitious enough to matter, grounded enough to keep the trust that took a decade to build.
